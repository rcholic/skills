# USMLE Exam Structure

## Step 1 (Basic Science)

- **Purpose**: Tests understanding of basic biomedical sciences
- **Format**: 280 questions, 7 blocks of 40 questions each
- **Time**: 8 hours total (60 min per block + breaks)
- **Scoring**: Pass/Fail only (since January 2022)
- **Passing score**: 196 (three-digit equivalent)
- **When to take**: After M2 year (US MDs) or before/during clinical rotations (IMGs)

## Step 2 CK (Clinical Knowledge)

- **Purpose**: Tests clinical knowledge and patient management
- **Format**: 318 questions, 8 blocks of ~40 questions each
- **Time**: 9 hours total (60 min per block + breaks)
- **Scoring**: Three-digit score (200-300+ range)
- **Mean score**: ~245-250
- **When to take**: During M3/M4 year, before ERAS application (September)

## Step 3

- **Purpose**: Tests independent patient management
- **Format**: Day 1 (232 MCQs), Day 2 (180 MCQs + 13 CCS cases)
- **Time**: Two days, ~8 hours each
- **Scoring**: Three-digit score
- **When to take**: During or after residency (required for full license)
- **Note**: Some competitive residencies want Step 3 before match

## Timing Considerations

### US MD/DO Students
- Step 1: End of M2 year (April-June)
- Step 2 CK: M3 year or early M4 (before September ERAS)
- Step 3: During intern year

### IMGs
- Step 1: Complete before clinical rotations (no time limit on eligibility)
- Step 2 CK: As soon as possible after Step 1 (maximize score for competitiveness)
- Need ECFMG certification before matching
- Step 2 CK score is CRITICAL for IMG competitiveness
