# Photography -- Interior Design

## Agent's Role

Agent helps with: shot lists, timing, staging checklists, photo selection, photographer briefing.
Agent does NOT: take photos, edit photos.

## Shot List -- Airbnb/Rental (15-20 photos)

1. **Hero shot** -- best room, best angle, determines clicks
2. Living room -- 2 angles
3. Kitchen -- 1-2 shots
4. Each bedroom -- 1-2 each
5. Each bathroom -- 1 each
6. Outdoor space -- 1-2 if exists
7. Detail shots -- 2-3 (amenities, unique features)

## Lighting Timing

| Orientation | Best time |
|-------------|-----------|
| East-facing | Morning (9-11 AM) |
| West-facing | Late afternoon (3-5 PM) |
| North-facing | Midday, cloudy preferred |
| South-facing | Midday, watch harsh shadows |

**Always:** Turn on ALL lights, match bulb temperatures, avoid flash.

## Composition

**Corner shot:** Shoot from room corner for max depth. Camera at chest height (120-130cm).

| Include | Exclude |
|---------|---------|
| Windows (show light) | Toilets (unless hero) |
| Fresh flowers | Trash cans |
| Mirrors (double space) | Power cords |
| Books, neutral decor | Personal items |

## Pre-Shoot Checklist

**Day before:**
- [ ] Deep clean all surfaces
- [ ] Remove personal items
- [ ] Replace burnt bulbs
- [ ] Fresh linens

**Day of:**
- [ ] Take out trash, hide bins
- [ ] Close toilet lids
- [ ] Clear countertops
- [ ] Open blinds (unless glare)
- [ ] Turn on ALL lights

## Product Photography in Interiors

**Scale:** Always provide product dimensions. Product = 20-40% of frame, not dominant.

**Consistency:** Same lighting, same product angle across variations. Background changes, product stays constant.
